Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why should I measure my emissions?

    Global warming is becoming a huge problem around the world and Governments are starting to implement laws to lower CO2 emissions. To get ahead of the curve you can measure your own emissions and try to lower them to do your part.

  • What are greenhouse gas emissions?

    A greenhouse gas is a gas that absorbs and emits radiant energy within the thermal infrared range. Greenhouse gases cause the greenhouse effect. The primary greenhouse gases in Earth's atmosphere are water vapor, carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and ozone.

  • How does this application caculate my emissions?

    Emissions planner uses a dataset of all vehicles sold in Canada from the Government of Canada. Within the dataset there is emissions of CO2 in grams/km for each different vehicle. We use this number to calculate the total emissions for each trip.

  • What are the primary sources of CO2 in our atmosphere?

    There are both natural and human sources of carbon dioxide emissions. Natural sources include decomposition, ocean release and respiration. Human sources come from activities like cement production, deforestation as well as the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas.

  • How do I reduce my CO2 emissions?

    There are many ways for a person to recude their CO2 emissions. The goal of Emission Planner is to make a person more aware of their C02 emissions when traveling and to provide alternate travel methods that are more eco-friendly.
